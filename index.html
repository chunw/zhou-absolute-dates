Abstract

Establishing the absolute dates of Western Zhou is a classical problem of ancient Chinese history that has remained unresolved for over two millennia. Here, I report a successful reconstruction of this long-sought chronology using archaeoastronomical methods. Systematic analysis of the relative compatibility of fully-dated bronze inscriptions revealed the definitions of the lunar phase terms, which enabled the computation of absolute dates. These calculations, combined with textual evidence, produced an absolute chronology starting in the year King Wen received the Mandate of Heaven and ending in the year of King Ping's death. The results reveal a previously unknown seven-year gap in the regnal years of Zhou, which reconciles evidence previously thought to be contradictory. The reconstructed chronology is highly consistent with multiple lines of evidence, thus providing a reliable temporal framework for studying ancient Chinese history, and facilitating a deeper understanding of the origins of Chinese civilization.


摘要

西周的确切年代困扰了中国史学界两千余年。近世以来，虽然出土了大量载有西周历日信息的金文，但是由于西周诸王在位时长、西周历法以及金文记日用语的含义等重要信息未知，使得西周年代仍难有定论。针对这种状况，本文通过分析八十一条四要素俱全的金文历日的相互关系，推断得出金文记日用语最合理的理解方式。在此基础上，结合文献的记载，可以编排上起文王受命元年、下至平王崩年的完整周王年代。这份年代方案与出土材料、文献资料以及古代天象均能够高度契合，是与全部已知信息最相符的结果，并且可以由新发现的材料检验和修正。希望本文的方法与结论能够为西周年代学研究提供有益的参考。
